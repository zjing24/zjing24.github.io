About me

I am a first-year Ph.D. student in Computer Sciences at the University of Wisconsin–Madison. I am grateful to be advised by Prof. Ilias Diakonikolas . I received my B.S. degree in Computer Science and Mathematics from the University of Wisconsin–Madison.

Research Interests

I am broadly interested in learning theory. During my undergrad, I worked on projects involving robustness and kernelized Stein discrepancy. I am currently working on problems in distribution testing, with the goal of obtaining optimal sample complexities under relaxations of the i.i.d. assumption.

I am also interested in algorithmic information theory and its applications. During my undergrad, I worked on applications of Kolmogorov complexity to geometric measure theory. (an good intro: a paper by Jack and Neil Lutz ). I hope to explore applications of Kolmogorov complexity in other areas as well.
